古典音乐图谱

A chronological listening path through sacred sound, opera, symphony, recording, cinema, games, and contemporary composition.

How to Listen / 如何使用

Start with three to five basic tracks in each chapter, then return to the full playlist for advanced works. The guide is not a ranking of famous pieces; it is a map of how musical language, institutions, technologies, and listening habits changed over time.

每章先听三到五首基础曲目，再回到完整歌单补充进阶作品。这不是名曲排行榜，而是一张关于音乐语言、制度、技术和听众习惯如何变化的地图。

Listening Tip
Listen first for texture: one line, melody plus accompaniment, or many independent voices.
Fun Fact
A work title can describe a genre, a nickname, a movement, or a catalog entry. Catalog numbers often search better than nicknames.
Asset Rule
This sample bundles no third-party images. Add portraits or artworks only after asset-manifest review.

Chapter Sample / 章节样本

Baroque / 巴洛克

Baroque music gives later European music several durable tools: ground bass, concerto dialogue, fugue, dance suite, and opera as public drama. Beginners can listen for the bass line first, then notice how ornaments and repeating patterns create motion.

巴洛克音乐为后来的欧洲音乐留下了几种持久工具：低音通奏、协奏曲对话、赋格、舞曲组曲，以及作为公共戏剧的歌剧。初学者可以先听低音线，再听装饰音和重复音型如何制造推进感。

Entry Works / 入门曲目
Vivaldi: The Four Seasons; Bach: Brandenburg Concerto No. 3; Handel: Messiah; Purcell: Dido and Aeneas.
